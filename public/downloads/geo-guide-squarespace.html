What This Guide Does

This guide walks Squarespace users through every GEO fix available on the platform. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) means making your site readable, trustworthy, and citable by AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Bing Copilot.

Squarespace gives you solid control over titles, meta descriptions, Open Graph tags, robots.txt, and schema injection via Code Injection. It does not support dynamic schema or full robots.txt editing natively, but the workarounds are straightforward.

Step 1 — Fix Your Homepage Title and Meta Description

Your homepage title is the first signal AI systems use to understand your site. It must be specific and descriptive.

How to update in Squarespace

  1. Go to Pages in the left sidebar
  2. Hover over your homepage and click the gear icon (Settings)
  3. Click the SEO tab
  4. Update the SEO Title field
  5. Update the SEO Description field
  6. Click Save

Also update your site-wide title

  1. Go to Settings → SEO → SEO Site Title
  2. Set your site title to your exact business name
  3. Save

Title formula

[What the page covers] | [Brand Name]

Example: AI GEO Visibility Audits | MyGeoRadar

Meta description formula

What do you offer, who is it for, what result do they get? Keep it 150–160 characters.

Step 2 — Add SEO Titles and Descriptions to Every Key Page

Every important page needs its own unique title and description. Repeat the process from Step 1 for each page.

Pages to update in order

  1. Homepage
  2. Services or product pages
  3. About page
  4. Contact page
  5. Pricing page
  6. Any blog index page

For blog posts

  1. Open any published blog post
  2. Click the gear icon → SEO tab
  3. Fill in the SEO Title and SEO Description
  4. Save

Step 3 — Inject Schema Markup via Code Injection

Squarespace does not have a built-in schema tool, but you can inject JSON-LD schema directly into the site header using Code Injection. This is the most important technical GEO fix on Squarespace.

Step 3A — Add Organization schema to your homepage

  1. Go to Settings → Advanced → Code Injection
  2. In the Header box, paste the following (fill in your details):
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Organization",
  "name": "Your Business Name",
  "url": "https://yoursite.com",
  "logo": "https://yoursite.com/logo.png",
  "description": "One sentence describing what your business does.",
  "email": "hello@yoursite.com",
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.linkedin.com/company/your-company",
    "https://twitter.com/yourhandle"
  ]
}
</script>
  1. Click Save

Step 3B — Add FAQPage schema to pages with FAQ sections

  1. Open the specific page in the editor
  2. Go to Page Settings → Advanced → Page Header Code Injection
  3. Paste your FAQPage JSON-LD:
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "What is GEO optimization?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "GEO optimization means structuring your website so AI tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity can read, trust, and cite it in their answers."
      }
    }
  ]
}
</script>
  1. Save the page

Verify your schema

  1. Go to Google Rich Results Test
  2. Enter your page URL
  3. Confirm schema appears with no errors

Step 4 — Enable and Configure Open Graph Tags

Open Graph tags control how your pages appear when shared on social media and are used by AI crawlers as metadata signals.

How to set Open Graph in Squarespace

  1. Go to Pages → hover over the page → click the gear icon
  2. Click the SEO tab
  3. Fill in the SEO Title and SEO Description (these also populate OG tags)
  4. Upload a Social Sharing Image (1200x630px recommended)
  5. Save

Set a default social sharing image for the whole site

  1. Go to Settings → SEO → Social Sharing
  2. Upload a default image (used when no page-specific image is set)
  3. Save

Step 5 — Fix Your Blog for GEO

Blog content is your highest-value GEO asset. AI systems actively pull from well-structured blog posts when forming answers.

Blog post structure for GEO

  1. Title (H1) — the question or topic
  2. Opening paragraph — direct 2–3 sentence answer (write this first)
  3. H2 section — background or context
  4. H2 section — step-by-step or detailed breakdown
  5. H2 section — FAQ or common questions
  6. Short closing paragraph
The opening paragraph matters most. AI systems often pull from the first 100 words of a post. Write a direct, quotable answer immediately after the title.

Step 6 — Add Author Information

AI systems favor content written by identified, credible authors. Squarespace shows the author name on blog posts by default.

How to update your author profile

  1. Go to Settings → Account → Profile
  2. Fill in your Display Name (use your real name)
  3. Add a Bio — 2–4 sentences describing your expertise
  4. Upload a Profile Photo
  5. Save

Make sure author name shows on posts

  1. Go to Pages → Blog Settings (gear icon on your blog page)
  2. Make sure Author is toggled on in the post meta display settings
  3. Save

Step 7 — Fix Image Alt Text

Alt text helps AI crawlers and screen readers understand what images contain. Squarespace lets you add alt text to every image block.

How to add alt text in Squarespace

  1. Click on any image in the editor to select it
  2. Click the pencil (edit) icon
  3. Find the Alt Text or Accessibility field
  4. Write a clear description of the image
  5. Save the block

Alt text rules

  • Describe what is shown in the image
  • Include product, service, or context when relevant
  • Keep it under 125 characters
  • Never duplicate alt text across images

Step 8 — Configure robots.txt

Squarespace provides a limited robots.txt editor. The most important thing is making sure AI crawlers are NOT blocked.

How to access robots.txt in Squarespace

  1. Go to Settings → Advanced → External Services
  2. Look for the Robots.txt or crawl settings option
  3. Make sure no important pages or directories are blocked
Critical: Do not block AI crawlers. Make sure your robots.txt does NOT contain Disallow: / for user agents like GPTBot or PerplexityBot.

Check your live robots.txt

  1. Visit yoursite.com/robots.txt in your browser
  2. Confirm it does not block major crawlers
  3. If it blocks everything, go back to External Services and update

Step 9 — Build Strong Trust Pages

Your About and Contact pages are trust signals that AI systems use to verify your business is real and legitimate.

About page — must include

  • Your name or founder name
  • What the business does
  • Who it helps
  • Where you are based
  • A real origin story (2–3 sentences minimum)
  • Your credentials or experience in this area
  • Contact info or link to contact page

Contact page — must include

  • Email address
  • Phone number (if applicable)
  • Physical address or service area
  • Contact form
  • Social links

NAP consistency rule

Your business Name, Address, and Phone must match exactly across your Squarespace site, Google Business Profile, Bing Places, and any directory listings. Inconsistencies reduce AI citation likelihood.

Step 10 — Build Off-Site Citations and Reviews

AI tools cross-reference your website against other trusted sources. Consistent, credible mentions of your business across the web increase citation likelihood.

Priority listings to create

  1. Google Business Profile — business.google.com — free and essential
  2. Bing Places for Business — bingplaces.com — feeds Bing/Copilot directly
  3. Trustpilot — create a profile and ask customers to leave text reviews
  4. LinkedIn Company Page — link from your site footer
  5. Better Business Bureau — high trust signal for AI
  6. Niche directories — find 2–3 industry-specific directories
