Introduction: The Search Revolution Nobody Told You About

Something fundamental changed in how people find businesses — and most business owners have no idea it happened.

When someone searches for “best dentist in Chicago” or “top-rated plumber near me” today, a growing percentage of them aren’t typing that into Google. They’re asking ChatGPT. They’re asking Perplexity. They’re asking Gemini or Claude. And those AI assistants don’t give them a list of links — they give them a name. Usually two or three names. Confidently. Specifically. Without sending the user anywhere else.

If your business isn’t one of those names, you lost a customer. Silently. Without a trace in your analytics.

The scale of the shift
Studies tracking consumer behavior in 2025–2026 consistently show that 55–65% of consumers now use an AI assistant as part of their research process before making a purchase or hiring a service provider. For high-consideration decisions — choosing a lawyer, dentist, contractor, or accountant — that number is even higher.

Traditional SEO took years to master and even longer to see results. The good news about AI visibility is that the signals are far more fixable, far faster. A business with zero AI presence today can have meaningful AI visibility within 30 days of following the steps in this playbook. We’ve seen businesses go from never being mentioned to being the first recommendation in their category within 6 weeks.

This playbook covers every signal AI assistants use to decide which businesses to mention, in exactly the order you should tackle them, with copy-paste templates and zero vague advice. Let’s get started.

How AI Assistants Actually Work

Why This Is Completely Different From SEO

Traditional SEO is a ranking game. You optimize a webpage, Google’s algorithm assigns it a position for a given keyword, and users click through to your site. The entire model depends on the click.

AI search has no ranking and no click. When someone asks ChatGPT “who should I hire for kitchen renovation in Denver?” the model generates a response from its internal knowledge and (in some cases) live web data. That response names businesses. The user acts on it. Your website may never be visited at all. You were either mentioned — or you weren’t.

The Three Questions Every AI Asks About Your Business

Before any AI assistant will confidently mention your business, it needs to answer three questions from the data it has access to:

1

Does this business clearly exist?

Entity clarity. The AI needs to know your business is a distinct, real, currently operating entity — not ambiguous, not defunct, not a duplicate. Structured data, a claimed GBP, and consistent directory listings are the primary signals here. Without clarity, the AI won’t risk mentioning you.

2

Is this business credible?

Trust signals. Has this business been mentioned in authoritative, independent sources? Does it have verified reviews? Is it cited consistently across multiple platforms? AI models weight businesses that appear in editorial sources, review platforms, and established directories. Volume and source quality both matter.

3

Is this business relevant to this query?

Attribute matching. Does what the AI knows about your business match what the user is asking for — the right category, location, specialty, and service type? A plumber who specializes in emergency services needs that specialty clearly documented — not just implied on a homepage.

How ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude Each Work Differently

Not all AI assistants are equal when it comes to how they find and cite businesses. Understanding the differences helps you prioritize which signals matter most.

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

ChatGPT’s base model relies primarily on its training data — a snapshot of the internet that OpenAI updates periodically. When ChatGPT (without browsing enabled) mentions a business, it’s drawing on information that was on the web during its last training cutoff. This means older but authoritative sources — long-established directory listings, press mentions, Wikipedia/Wikidata entries — carry significant weight. ChatGPT with browsing enabled behaves more like Perplexity. The key for ChatGPT: establish a deep, consistent, long-term entity footprint across authoritative sources.

Perplexity

Perplexity performs a live web search before generating every response. This means it’s pulling fresh data — your current website, recent reviews, live directory listings. This makes Perplexity the fastest to respond to changes you make. If you fix your GBP and add schema today, Perplexity can reflect that change within days to weeks. The key for Perplexity: have clean, crawlable, well-structured current web presence.

Gemini (Google)

Gemini has direct access to Google’s Knowledge Graph — arguably the most comprehensive business entity database in the world. Your Google Business Profile data feeds directly into what Gemini knows about you. This makes GBP optimization the single highest-leverage action for Gemini visibility. Gemini also blends training data with live search, similar to Perplexity. The key for Gemini: GBP completeness, Google reviews, and Google-indexed structured data.

Claude (Anthropic)

Claude relies primarily on training data and is generally the most conservative of the four — it will often decline to make specific local business recommendations rather than risk being wrong. To appear in Claude’s responses, you need authoritative written sources — editorial mentions, clearly written content on your own website, and Wikidata entries. The key for Claude: editorial credibility and clearly written, factual website content.

The unified strategy
The good news: the actions in this playbook improve your visibility across all four engines simultaneously. You don’t need a different strategy for each one. The foundation (GBP, schema, directories) helps all of them. The authority layer (About page, /facts page, editorial mentions) pushes Claude and ChatGPT. The freshness layer (GSC, active GBP, new content) helps Perplexity and Gemini.

Part 1 — The Foundation
Steps 1–3: Entity Clarity

These three steps are non-negotiable. Do not skip ahead to the authority layer without completing these first. The foundation is what makes you findable. Everything else makes you recommendable.

Step 1: Claim and Complete Your Google Business Profile

Impact: Very High — affects Gemini directly, and all others indirectly through the Knowledge Graph.

Google’s Knowledge Graph is the foundational data layer that multiple AI systems draw from. A fully completed, verified GBP is the single highest-leverage action most local businesses can take for AI visibility.

How to claim your GBP

  1. Go to business.google.com and sign in with a Google account
  2. Search for your business name — if it exists as an unverified listing, claim it; if it doesn’t, create a new one
  3. Choose your primary business category (be specific — see guidance below)
  4. Verify via the method Google offers (postcard, phone call, video, or instant verification for some categories)

Every field you must complete (and exactly how)

Business Name

Use your exact legal business name. Do not add keywords (e.g., “Best Denver Plumber — Peak Plumbing Co.”) — Google will suspend listings that keyword-stuff the name field, and keyword stuffing actually confuses AI entity matching.

Primary Category

This is the single most important field in your GBP for AI relevance matching. Choose the most specific category that accurately describes your primary service. Examples:

  • Not “Contractor” → use “General Contractor” or “Kitchen Remodeler”
  • Not “Doctor” → use “Family Practice Physician” or “Pediatrician”
  • Not “Lawyer” → use “Personal Injury Attorney” or “Family Law Attorney”
  • Not “Restaurant” → use “Italian Restaurant” or “Sushi Restaurant”

Secondary Categories

Add 2–4 secondary categories that represent services you actually offer. These expand the queries you can match. A dental practice might add: Cosmetic Dentist, Orthodontist, Emergency Dental Service.

Business Description (750 characters — use all of them)

This is the field AI systems are most likely to quote. Use this exact formula:

GBP Description Formula

[Business name] is a [specific business type] in [city, state] specializing in [primary service], [secondary service], and [tertiary service]. We serve [customer type/demographic] in [specific service area or neighborhoods]. [One sentence on what makes you distinctly credible — years in business, certifications, awards, or notable specialization.] [One sentence on your approach or values if space permits.]

Example: Lakeview Dental is a family and cosmetic dental practice in Chicago, Illinois specializing in Invisalign, dental implants, and emergency dental care. We serve families and adults throughout the Lincoln Park, Lakeview, and Wrigleyville neighborhoods. Dr. Sarah Chen has 18 years of experience and is a certified Invisalign Diamond Provider with over 800 completed cases. We offer same-day emergency appointments six days a week.

Phone Number

Use a local phone number, not an 800 number. The area code is an entity signal that confirms your geographic location to AI systems.

Website

Link to your homepage, not a tracking URL or landing page. The domain should match your business name or be a clear brand domain.

Address

Enter exactly as it appears on official documents. Write down this exact format — you’ll use it on every directory in Step 3.

Hours

Enter your real hours. Keep them updated. Outdated hours are one of the most common causes of AI hallucinations (AI reporting wrong hours to potential customers).

Services / Products Section

Add every service you offer. For each service, write a 1–2 sentence description. This section significantly expands the range of queries you can match in AI answers.

Photos

Upload minimum 10 photos. AI systems do not “see” photos, but photo completeness is a proxy signal for listing quality. Include: exterior (2), interior (2), team/owner (2), work samples or products (3+), logo (1).

Reviews

You cannot fake this — but you can ask for it. After every completed job or service, send a follow-up text or email with your direct GBP review link (find it in your GBP dashboard under “Get more reviews”). A business with 50+ recent, positive reviews is dramatically more likely to be recommended by AI than one with 5 reviews from 3 years ago.

After completing your GBP
Wait 48–72 hours before moving to Step 3 (directories). This gives Google time to propagate your updated information. Submitting directories before GBP is indexed can create conflicting data.

Step 2: Add Schema Markup to Your Website

Impact: High — affects all four AI engines. Provides machine-readable structured data that AI crawlers consume directly.

Schema markup is JSON-LD code you add to your website that tells AI crawlers exactly who you are, what you do, where you are, and what you offer. Without it, AI systems infer your business details from unstructured text on your pages. With it, they read structured facts from a source you control.

How to add schema markup (no coding required)

Every schema block is a <script type="application/ld+json"> tag you paste inside the <head> of the relevant page. On most platforms:

  • WordPress: Install the free “Schema & Structured Data for WP & AMP” plugin, or paste into your theme’s header.php
  • Squarespace: Settings → Advanced → Code Injection → Header
  • Wix: Settings → Custom Code → Add to <head>
  • Shopify: Online Store → Themes → Edit Code → theme.liquid, paste before </head>
  • Webflow: Page Settings → Custom Code → Head Code

Part 2 — The Authority Layer
Steps 4–7: Credibility & Content

The foundation makes you findable. The authority layer makes you recommendable. These steps create the content and signals that AI systems quote directly when describing your business.

Step 4: Set Up Google Search Console and Submit Your Sitemap

Impact: Medium-High — accelerates indexing, surfaces crawl errors, ensures AI systems can read your site.

Setup (10 minutes)

  1. Go to search.google.com/search-console and sign in
  2. Click “Add property” and enter your domain (e.g., yourwebsite.com)
  3. Verify ownership using the HTML tag method: copy the meta tag, paste it into your homepage <head>, then click Verify in GSC
  4. Once verified, go to Sitemaps in the left menu
  5. Enter your sitemap URL (usually yourwebsite.com/sitemap.xml) and click Submit
  6. Request indexing of your most important pages: go to URL Inspection, enter each key page URL, and click “Request Indexing”

If you don’t have a sitemap

  • WordPress: Install Yoast SEO (free) — it creates a sitemap automatically at yoursite.com/sitemap_index.xml
  • Squarespace / Wix / Webflow: Auto-generated at yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml
  • Other: Generate one free at xml-sitemaps.com

What to monitor monthly

  • Coverage report: Check for pages marked “Excluded” or “Error” — these pages are invisible to AI
  • Core Web Vitals: Slow pages get crawled less frequently
  • Security issues: A hacked or flagged site will be deprioritized or removed from AI sources entirely

Step 5: Rewrite Your About Page So AI Can Quote It

Impact: High for ChatGPT and Claude — these engines heavily weight your About page for entity information.

Most About pages are written for humans and are full of vague claims like “we’re passionate about our customers” and “we’ve been serving the community for years.” AI systems need dense, quotable facts — specific numbers, dates, names, and credentials.

The AI-Optimized About Page Structure

Paragraph 1 — The What (2–3 sentences): State exactly what your business is, where it is, and what it primarily does. Include city, state, founding year, and primary service. This is what AI will quote directly.

Paragraph 2 — The Who (2–3 sentences): Name the founder or key person, their credentials, years of experience, and relevant background. First and last name. Specific certifications. Specific numbers.

Paragraph 3 — The Proof (2–3 sentences): Specific achievements — number of clients served, years in operation, awards, publications, notable projects, or affiliations with recognized organizations.

Paragraph 4 — The Service Detail (3–5 sentences): A complete list of your services, service area, customer types, and any specializations. The more specific, the better.

Paragraph 5 — The Contact Summary: Address, phone, email, and hours in plain text — not just in a contact form. AI crawlers often can’t read form fields.

Example of a bad About page (before)

Before — AI cannot quote this
“We’re a family-owned business that truly cares about our customers. Our team of dedicated professionals has years of experience helping people in the local area. We pride ourselves on quality work and excellent service. Contact us today to learn more!”

Example of a good About page (after)

After — AI can quote every sentence
“Peak Plumbing Co. is a licensed residential and commercial plumbing company founded in 2009 in Denver, Colorado. The company specializes in emergency pipe repair, water heater installation, and whole-home repiping, serving homeowners and property managers throughout the Denver metro area. Owner and master plumber David Reyes holds Colorado State License #MP-00124 and has completed over 4,200 plumbing projects in 15 years of operation. Peak Plumbing is a member of the Plumbing-Heating-Cooling Contractors Association and has maintained an A+ rating with the Better Business Bureau since 2012. The company serves Denver, Aurora, Lakewood, Englewood, and Littleton.”

Step 7: Build a FAQ Page with FAQPage Schema

Impact: High — FAQPage schema is one of the most directly cited data sources across all AI engines.

A properly structured FAQ page with FAQPage schema is one of the highest-value pages you can have for AI visibility. AI assistants are literally trained to answer questions — and your FAQ page gives them pre-formatted, authoritative answers attributed to your business.

What makes a great AI-optimized FAQ page

  • Minimum 8 questions (10–15 is ideal)
  • Questions should match what people actually ask an AI about businesses in your category
  • Answers should be 2–4 complete sentences — long enough to be informative, short enough to quote directly
  • Include your business name, city, and specific services in the answers themselves
  • Use FAQPage schema (from Step 2 template) so AI crawlers can read the Q&A structure directly

Question starters to use

  • “What does [Business Name] specialize in?”
  • “Is [Business Name] licensed and insured?”
  • “How much does [primary service] typically cost?”
  • “What areas does [Business Name] serve?”
  • “How long has [Business Name] been in business?”
  • “What makes [Business Name] different from other [business type]s?”
  • “Do you offer emergency/same-day service?”
  • “What are your payment options?”
  • “How do I get a quote from [Business Name]?”
  • “Are your [staff/products/ingredients] [licensed/certified/local/organic]?”

Part 3 — The Credibility Layer
Steps 8–10: Authority Signals

The credibility layer is what separates businesses that are occasionally mentioned by AI from businesses that are consistently recommended. These steps build the third-party authority signals that AI systems use to make confident, positive endorsements.

Step 8: Get Your First Editorial Mention

Impact: Very High for ChatGPT and Claude — editorial mentions from established publications are weighted heavily as credibility signals.

An “editorial mention” means your business is named in an article, blog post, podcast, or news story by a third party — not paid advertising, not a directory listing you control. It’s someone else saying you exist and are worth mentioning.

The 5 best sources for a first editorial mention

1. Local newspaper or news website

Most cities have a local newspaper or news site that covers business openings, community stories, and local profiles. Email their business desk with a genuine story angle — a business anniversary, a community initiative, an unusual service, a local hiring expansion. News sites have high domain authority and are widely indexed by AI systems.

Local press pitch email template

Subject: Story idea: [Business Name] — [brief compelling angle]

Hi [Reporter’s Name],

I’m [Your Name], owner of [Business Name] in [City]. I wanted to share a story that might interest your readers: [1–2 sentence story angle — why is this interesting or relevant to your community right now?].

[Business Name] [one key fact that makes the story newsworthy — years in business, community contribution, unique service, milestone].

Happy to provide photos, background information, or a quick phone call. Would this be a fit for [Publication Name]?

Best,
[Your Name]
[Business Name] | [Phone] | [Email]

2. Industry blog or trade publication

Most industries have trade publications or popular blogs that accept guest posts or feature member businesses. A 600–800 word guest article that provides genuine value to their readers is a high-authority editorial mention that will be indexed by AI systems for years.

3. Local business journal

Business journals like the Denver Business Journal, Chicago Business, or Nashville Business Journal regularly feature local business profiles. These have exceptionally high domain authority and are widely cited by AI systems when asked about local business recommendations.

4. Podcast interview

A podcast episode where you are the expert guest counts as an editorial mention if the episode has show notes on a website (which nearly all do). The key is that the show notes are indexed and link to your business. Find podcasts in your industry or local business podcasts and pitch yourself as a guest expert.

5. Chamber of Commerce or Association feature

If you’re a member of your local Chamber of Commerce, many chambers feature member businesses in their newsletter, blog, or website directory. This is easy to obtain and creates a high-trust citation from an established organization.

Step 9: Index Your Site in Bing Webmaster Tools

Impact: Medium-High specifically for Microsoft Copilot — Bing’s index directly powers Copilot answers.

Most businesses focus on Google and completely ignore Bing. This is a mistake in the AI era because Microsoft Copilot — integrated into Windows, Edge, and Microsoft 365 — draws directly from Bing’s index. Copilot has hundreds of millions of users.

Setup (15 minutes)

  1. Go to bing.com/webmasters and sign in with a Microsoft account
  2. Add your site and verify ownership (use the XML file method or meta tag — both work)
  3. Submit your sitemap URL
  4. Go to URL Submission and submit your most important pages manually (homepage, About, FAQ, /facts)
  5. Make sure your Bing Places listing (Step 3) is linked to the same domain

Bonus: Also submit to IndexNow

IndexNow is a protocol supported by Bing, Yandex, and several other engines that lets you instantly notify them when a page is created or updated. Submitting to IndexNow after any website change means your updates reach Bing (and Copilot) within hours instead of weeks. Instructions at indexnow.org.

Step 10: Create a Wikidata Entry

Impact: High for ChatGPT and Claude — Wikidata is one of the most trusted structured knowledge sources that AI systems draw from.

Wikidata is a free, open-access knowledge base maintained by the Wikimedia Foundation (the same organization behind Wikipedia). Unlike Wikipedia, which requires articles about “notable” subjects, Wikidata accepts entries for any business or organization. It is structured data — exactly the kind of authoritative, machine-readable information that AI systems prioritize.

How to create your Wikidata entry

  1. Create a free account at wikidata.org
  2. Click “Create a new item” in the left menu
  3. Add your business name as the label (in English) and a 1-sentence description (e.g., “plumbing company based in Denver, Colorado”)
  4. Add the following statements (properties):

    • instance of (P31): business (Q4830453) or the most specific applicable type
    • country (P17): United States of America (Q30)
    • located in (P131): Your city (search for it)
    • official website (P856): Your URL
    • inception (P571): Your founding year
    • industry (P452): Your industry
    • Google Knowledge Graph ID (P2671): Find yours by searching your business on Google and inspecting the URL that appears when you click “Feedback” on the knowledge panel
  5. Save. Your entry will be reviewed and live within 24–48 hours.
Why this matters so much
AI systems like ChatGPT are specifically trained to use Wikidata as a structured facts source. A Wikidata entry is the closest thing to “registering” your business in an AI directory. Many businesses that appear in ChatGPT answers have Wikidata entries. Most businesses that are completely absent from ChatGPT answers do not.

Troubleshooting: When AI Gets Your Business Wrong

AI systems sometimes generate confidently wrong information about businesses — incorrect hours, outdated addresses, wrong services, or inaccurate descriptions. Here’s how to find it and fix it.

Step 1: Audit What AI Is Currently Saying About You

Open each of the four AI assistants and ask these questions about your business. Screenshot every response.

  • “Tell me everything you know about [Business Name] in [City, State].”
  • “What services does [Business Name] offer and what are their hours?”
  • “Is [Business Name] a reputable choice for [your service category] in [city]?”
  • “What do customers say about [Business Name]?”

Note every piece of wrong, missing, or outdated information.

Step 2: Identify the Source of the Wrong Information

Wrong AI information usually originates from one of three sources:

  • An outdated directory listing — an old Yelp listing with a former address, a Yellow Pages entry with an old phone number
  • Old website content — a page that used to exist and was indexed before you updated it
  • A data aggregator — companies like Data Axle and Localeze aggregate and distribute business data to hundreds of directories. If they have wrong data, it spreads everywhere. You can submit corrections at infogroup.com and whitepages.com/business

Step 3: The Fix — Authoritative Signal Flooding

You cannot directly edit what an AI says about you. But you can flood the web with accurate, authoritative signals that overwhelm and replace the wrong data. The process:

  1. Fix every directory listing (use the 10 core from Step 3 as your checklist)
  2. Update your schema markup with accurate current information
  3. Update your GBP, especially hours and categories
  4. Add a “Corrections” note to your /facts page if the wrong information is specifically addressed there
  5. For Perplexity: submit your corrected pages via their feedback mechanism
  6. For ChatGPT: submit corrections through OpenAI’s business information form (platform.openai.com/docs/contact)
