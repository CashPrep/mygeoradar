1. The Shift

What changed, why it matters, and why most businesses are blind to it.

When someone types "best dentist near me" into ChatGPT, they don't get ten blue links. They get a name. Two or three names. With reasons. The AI sounds confident. The user acts on it.

Your Google ranking doesn't matter in that moment. Your website traffic doesn't matter. What matters is one thing: did the AI mention your business?

This is the new customer acquisition channel — and it operates invisibly. You won't see a referral in your analytics. You'll just slowly lose customers you never knew you were competing for.

The opportunity: Most businesses have done zero to optimize for AI visibility. The ones who act now will own AI recommendations in their category before competitors wake up.

2. The 3-Question Framework

Before any AI mentions your business, it silently asks three questions.

1
Does this business clearly exist?

Entity clarity. The AI needs consistent, structured data confirming you're a real operating business — not a duplicate or closed listing. Solved by GBP + schema + citations.

2
Is this business credible?

Trust signals. Reviews, press mentions, directory listings. Volume and source quality both matter.

3
Is this business relevant to this query?

Attribute matching. Does what AI knows about you match what the user asked — right category, location, specialty? Vague descriptions get skipped. Specific ones get cited.

Everything in this playbook answers "yes" to all three. That's the whole game.

3. How Each AI Engine Actually Works

They're not the same. Knowing the difference changes what you prioritize.

ChatGPT (GPT-4o)

Primarily training data. Relies on what it learned during training. High-authority sources — Wikipedia, directories, press coverage, Google-indexed structured data — carry the most weight.

Perplexity

Live web search every query. Your current website, recent reviews, and new press mentions show up quickly. Being well-indexed by Google and Bing is critical here.

Gemini (Google)

Deeply integrated with Google's Knowledge Graph. Your GBP, Google Maps data, and Google-indexed content are primary signals. Gemini has near-direct access to everything Google knows about you.

Claude (Anthropic)

Training data focused. More conservative — prefers authoritative long-form content. Industry publications, detailed About pages, and structured guides help most here.

One system covers all four: GBP + schema covers Gemini. Directory citations + press mentions cover ChatGPT and Claude. Fresh indexed content covers Perplexity.

Step 1 — Google Business Profile

The single highest-leverage action. Do this before anything else.

Google's Knowledge Graph feeds Gemini directly, shapes ChatGPT's training data, and is a top source for Perplexity's live results. A complete, claimed GBP is non-negotiable.

Exactly What To Do

1
Claim your listing at business.google.com

Search your business name. Claim it if it exists, create it if not. Verify via postcard, phone, or video.

2
Set your primary category precisely

"Family Law Attorney" not "Lawyer." "Italian Restaurant" not "Restaurant." Specificity determines which AI queries you appear in.

3
Add 2–3 secondary categories

Expands query types you're matched to without diluting your primary signal.

4
Write your description using this formula:
[Business Name] is a [specific type] in [City, State] specializing in [service 1], [service 2], and [service 3]. We serve [customer type] across [service area]. [One sentence on what makes you notable: years, award, certification].

Example: Riverside Legal is a family law firm in Austin, Texas specializing in divorce, child custody, and adoption. We serve individuals and families throughout Travis and Williamson County. Our attorneys have 20+ combined years of experience and are board-certified by the Texas Board of Legal Specialization.

5
Fill every single field

Address, local phone number, website, hours (including holidays), services list, attributes (wheelchair accessible, woman-owned, etc.), and at least 10 photos.

Watch for duplicates: Search your business name + city on Google Maps. If more than one listing appears, the duplicate splits your authority and confuses AI. Request removal via Google Business support chat.

Step 2 — Schema Markup

The code that tells AI exactly who you are. Copy, fill, paste.

Schema converts your webpage into machine-readable facts. AI crawlers read it directly — no inference needed. Without schema, AI is guessing. With it, AI reads a source you control.

LocalBusiness Schema — paste into homepage head

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "YOUR BUSINESS NAME",
  "description": "YOUR GBP DESCRIPTION",
  "url": "https://yourwebsite.com",
  "telephone": "+1-XXX-XXX-XXXX",
  "email": "hello@yoursite.com",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main Street",
    "addressLocality": "Your City",
    "addressRegion": "XX",
    "postalCode": "00000",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": 00.0000,
    "longitude": -00.0000
  },
  "openingHoursSpecification": [{
    "@type": "OpeningHoursSpecification",
    "dayOfWeek": ["Monday","Tuesday","Wednesday","Thursday","Friday"],
    "opens": "09:00",
    "closes": "17:00"
  }],
  "priceRange": "$$",
  "areaServed": "Your City, Your State",
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.facebook.com/yourbusiness",
    "https://www.yelp.com/biz/your-business"
  ]
}

Use your specific type instead of "LocalBusiness" for stronger signals: Restaurant, MedicalBusiness, LegalService, HomeAndConstructionBusiness, HealthAndBeautyBusiness, AutomotiveBusiness, FinancialService, RealEstateAgent. Full list at schema.org/LocalBusiness

FAQPage Schema — paste into About or FAQ page

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "What services does [Business] offer?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "[Business] offers [list your services clearly]."
      }
    },
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "Where is [Business] located and what areas do you serve?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "We are located at [address] and serve [cities/regions]."
      }
    },
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "How long has [Business] been in business?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "[Business] has been serving [city] since [year]."
      }
    },
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "How do I contact [Business]?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Call us at [phone], email [email], or visit [website]."
      }
    }
  ]
}

How To Add It (No Developer Needed)

  • WordPress: Install free plugin "Schema & Structured Data for WP & AMP" — paste JSON directly
  • Squarespace / Wix: Settings → Advanced → Code Injection → paste in the head block
  • Shopify: Online Store → Themes → Edit Code → theme.liquid → paste before </head>
  • Any site: Wrap in <script type="application/ld+json">...</script> in your page head

Validate: search.google.com/test/rich-results — paste your URL. Zero critical errors = you're good.

Step 3 — Citation Footprint

AI verifies you're real by finding you in multiple independent sources.

A citation is any online mention of your Name, Address, and Phone (NAP). The rule: every citation must be identical. "St." vs "Street" is enough variation to dilute your signal.

Define Your NAP Source of Truth First

[Business Legal Name] [Street Number] [Street Name], [Suite] [City], [State] [ZIP] Phone: (XXX) XXX-XXXX Website: https://yourwebsite.com

Write this down. Use it everywhere. Never deviate.

Step 4 — About & Facts Pages

AI crawls your About page looking for quotable facts. Most About pages fail completely.

Rewrite Your About Page With These 5 Paragraphs

  1. The What: One sentence. What your business is, what it does, where. Be specific and factual. Write for a machine first, a human second.
  2. The Who: Named customer types, named locations. "We serve homeowners in the Seattle metro area" beats "we serve clients across the region."
  3. The Services: Bullet list of exact services. Named, specific, no jargon.
  4. The Credentials: Years in business, certifications, awards, licenses, associations. Numbers and named credentials only.
  5. The Contact: Full address, phone, email, website in plain text — yes, on a webpage. AI reads it as structured fact.

Create a /facts Page

This is the most underused GEO tactic. Create yourwebsite.com/facts structured like a Wikipedia entry for your business:

  • Business name (legal and DBA)
  • Founded: [year], by [founder name]
  • Location: [full address]
  • Service area: [cities/regions]
  • Primary services: [bulleted list]
  • Team size / number of employees
  • Licenses and certifications with issuing bodies
  • Awards and recognitions with years
  • Media mentions with publication names and dates
  • Social profiles (linked)

Why it works: Perplexity and ChatGPT with browsing frequently cite /facts-style pages when generating business summaries because the information is dense, specific, and clearly structured. This becomes the "source of record" AI systems prefer over vague homepage copy.

Step 5 — Reviews

Reviews are a credibility signal for AI — not just for humans.

AI treats review volume and recency as trust signals. 47 reviews at 4.3 stars signals more confidence than 4 reviews at 5.0 stars. More data = more confident recommendation.

1
Build your direct review link

GBP dashboard → "Get more reviews" → copy the short link. Use it everywhere.

2
Ask at the right moment

Immediately after a positive interaction. Text works best: "Thanks for choosing us — if you have 30 seconds, an honest Google review helps others find us: [link]"

3
Spread across platforms

Google first, then Yelp, then Facebook. Don't mass-ask everyone on the same day — Google may filter reviews it suspects are coordinated.

Target: 25+ Google reviews at 4.2+ average. That's the threshold where AI systems begin treating you as confidently recommendable rather than an unknown quantity.

Step 6 — Your First Editorial Mention

This separates consistently recommended businesses from occasionally cited ones.

An editorial mention is when an independent, non-paid source references your business. AI weights these heavily because they represent third-party validation you can't fake with structured data alone.

5 Easiest Ways To Get Your First One

1
Local newspaper or news site

Pitch a genuine story: anniversary, unique service, community involvement. Email the local business reporter directly — not the general inbox.

2
Chamber of Commerce spotlight

Most chambers publish member spotlights on their website and newsletter. Join and proactively request a feature. Strong local authority signal.

3
Connectively (formerly HARO) — free

Sign up at connectively.us. Journalists post daily source requests. Filter for your industry. Respond with expert quotes. One national placement is worth dozens of directory citations for AI credibility.

4
Industry association listing

Trade associations publish member directories on high-authority domains. Join your relevant association and ensure your listing is complete. (NAR for real estate, NAHB for contractors, ADA for dentists, ABA for lawyers, etc.)

5
Guest post on a local or industry blog

Write a genuinely useful 600-word article. Include a byline with your name and business. This creates an authoritative, indexed citation AI can reference.

Step 7 — Fixing AI Hallucinations

When AI says something wrong about your business, here's the exact fix.

AI hallucinates when conflicting or incomplete data exists in your citation footprint. You can't edit what AI says directly — but you can flood the web with accurate signals until correct information dominates.

1
Audit what each AI says

Use Prompt #1 from your Prompt Pack: "Tell me everything you know about [Business Name] in [City]." Run in all four assistants. Screenshot every incorrect fact.

2
Find the source of bad data

Search your business name + the incorrect detail on Google. Find the citation with old/wrong information — usually an old Yelp or Yellow Pages entry.

3
Fix or remove the source

Log in and correct it. If you can't claim it, flag it for removal or contact the site directly.

4
Flood with correct information

Update GBP, schema, /facts page, and website footer with the accurate details. Correct information needs to outnumber the incorrect source by at least 5:1.

Timeline: Perplexity updates within days to weeks. ChatGPT and Claude take longer. Most hallucinations are corrected within 30–60 days of consistent signal work.

Step 8 — Tracking Your Progress

30 minutes per month. No expensive tools required.

One-Time Setup

  • Google Search Console: search.google.com/search-console → add your domain → verify ownership → submit sitemap at yoursite.com/sitemap.xml
  • Bing Webmaster Tools: bing.com/webmasters → import from Google Search Console (2 minutes) → activates Microsoft Copilot data sourcing

Monthly 30-Minute AI Audit

On the first of every month, run these in ChatGPT and Perplexity:

  • "What can you tell me about [Business Name] in [City]?"
  • "Who would you recommend for [your service] in [your city]?"
  • "Is [Business Name] a reputable choice for [your service] in [your city]?"

Log responses in a simple spreadsheet. Track: mentioned? accurate? recommended over competitors? This 30-minute habit shows you clearly whether your AI visibility is improving month over month.
