Section 1 — Critical Fixes

7 Issues AI Crawlers Cannot Ignore

Fix these first. Each one directly determines whether AI can read, trust, and cite your site.

1

Switch Your Site to HTTPS

Critical 🕑 15–30 minutes
Why This Matters
HTTPS is the single most basic trust signal on the web. AI crawlers like GPTBot and ClaudeBot treat HTTP sites the same way browsers do — they flag them as insecure and heavily deprioritise them. If your site is still on HTTP, AI assistants may skip it entirely when building their answers. This is the first thing to fix because everything else builds on top of it.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Log in to your hosting control panel (cPanel, Cloudflare, Vercel, Netlify, etc.).
  2. 2Find the SSL/TLS section. Most modern hosts offer a free Let’s Encrypt certificate — look for “Enable SSL”, “Force HTTPS”, or “Install Certificate”.
  3. 3Enable the certificate and turn on “Force HTTPS” or “Redirect HTTP to HTTPS”. This ensures anyone visiting http:// gets bounced to https:// automatically.
  4. 4If your host does not offer free SSL, add your domain to Cloudflare (free plan). Go to SSL/TLS → set mode to “Full”. Enable “Always Use HTTPS” under Edge Certificates.
  5. 5After enabling, visit your site at https://yourdomain.com and confirm the padlock icon appears in the browser address bar.
  6. 6Check for mixed content: open Chrome DevTools (F12) → Console tab. If you see warnings about insecure resources, those images or scripts are loading over HTTP. Update their URLs to https://.
  7. 7Update any hardcoded http:// links in your site’s code, CMS, or page builder to use https://.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
Go to ssllabs.com/ssltest/ and enter your domain. You should get an A or A+ rating. Then re-run your free scan at mygeoradar.com — the HTTPS check should turn green.
2

Make Your Site Publicly Accessible

Critical 🕑 10–60 minutes
Why This Matters
If AI crawlers cannot load your page, they cannot learn anything about your business. A site that times out, returns a 4xx/5xx error, or hides behind a login wall is invisible to every AI assistant — no matter how good your content is. This is the most critical check of all.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Open a fresh incognito/private browser window and visit your site URL. If it loads normally, the issue may be intermittent — check your hosting uptime monitor.
  2. 2Test externally: go to downforeveryoneorjustme.com and enter your URL. This confirms whether it’s your server or your connection.
  3. 3If your site is down, log in to your hosting dashboard and check for server errors, suspended accounts, or expired domains.
  4. 4If your site requires a login to view, add an exception for the homepage. In WordPress: Settings → Reading → uncheck “Discourage search engines”. Disable any Maintenance Mode or Coming Soon plugin.
  5. 5Check server response time. If pages take over 5 seconds to load, AI crawlers will time out. Contact your host about upgrading or add Cloudflare caching.
  6. 6Verify your domain is not expired: go to who.is and check the expiration date. Renew immediately if it’s within 30 days.
  7. 7If you use Cloudflare, confirm “Under Attack Mode” is OFF — this blocks crawlers with a JavaScript challenge they cannot pass.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
Go to reqbin.com, enter your URL, and click Send. You should see “200 OK” in the response status. Re-run your free scan — the reachable check should turn green.
3

Stop Blocking AI Crawlers in robots.txt

Critical 🕑 5–10 minutes
Why This Matters
Your robots.txt file tells crawlers what they’re allowed to access. If it blocks GPTBot, ClaudeBot, or all bots with a wildcard rule, AI assistants cannot read your site at all. Many business owners added these blocks years ago for privacy reasons without realising they were shutting out AI entirely. This fix takes under 5 minutes.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Visit https://yourdomain.com/robots.txt in your browser. You’ll see a plain text file.
  2. 2Look for any of these: “Disallow: /” under “User-agent: *”, “User-agent: GPTBot”, “User-agent: ClaudeBot”, “User-agent: Google-Extended”, “User-agent: CCBot”. These are the rules blocking AI.
  3. 3In WordPress, use the Yoast SEO plugin → SEO → Tools → File Editor to edit robots.txt. Or install the “Robots.txt Editor” plugin. On other platforms, find the file in your root directory via FTP or your host’s file manager.
  4. 4Remove the blocking rules. The safest approach: delete the AI bot User-agent blocks entirely.
  5. 5A clean, AI-friendly robots.txt looks like this:

    User-agent: *
    Allow: /

    Sitemap: https://yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml
  6. 6Save the file and visit https://yourdomain.com/robots.txt again to confirm the blocking rules are gone.
  7. 7Optionally, submit your sitemap to Google Search Console — it helps AI systems discover your content faster.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
Visit your robots.txt file and confirm no “Disallow: /” rules exist for * or AI bot user agents. Re-run your free scan — the robots.txt check should turn green.
4

Write a Strong Meta Title

Critical 🕑 10–15 minutes
Why This Matters
The meta title is the first thing AI crawlers read to understand what your page is about. A title that’s too short, too generic, or missing entirely means AI cannot confidently categorise your business — so it won’t mention you when someone asks about your industry. A well-written title takes 10 minutes to fix and has an immediate impact.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Use this formula: [Business Name] — [Primary Service] | [City or Niche]. Example: “Riverside HVAC — Heating & Cooling Repair | Austin, TX”
  2. 2Keep it between 30 and 65 characters. Shorter is too vague. Longer gets cut off in search results and AI summaries.
  3. 3Include your most important keyword naturally — what would a customer type into ChatGPT to find you? That phrase should be in your title.
  4. 4WordPress: Open Yoast SEO or RankMath on the page. Scroll to the SEO section and update the “SEO Title” field.
  5. 5Squarespace: Pages → click your page → gear icon → SEO → “SEO Title”.
  6. 6Shopify: Online Store → Preferences → Homepage title. For other pages: edit the page → scroll to “Search engine listing preview” → Edit.
  7. 7Custom HTML: find <title> in your <head> and update the text. Every page should have a unique title.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
Right-click your page → View Page Source → search for “<title>”. Confirm your new title appears. Re-run your free scan — the meta title check should be green.
5

Write a Clear Meta Description

Critical 🕑 10–20 minutes
Why This Matters
The meta description is a 1–2 sentence summary AI crawlers use to understand what your business does and who it serves. A missing or weak description means AI has to guess — and it will often get it wrong or skip you in favour of a competitor who explains themselves clearly.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Write a 120–155 character description that answers: What do you do? Who do you serve? Where are you (if local)?
  2. 2Local example: “Riverside HVAC provides heating, cooling, and air quality services to homeowners in Austin, TX. Licensed, same-day service available.” (152 chars)
  3. 3Online example: “PromptBase is a marketplace where you can buy and sell high-quality AI prompts for ChatGPT, Midjourney, DALL-E, and Stable Diffusion.” (143 chars)
  4. 4Avoid keyword stuffing. Write it as if explaining your business to a smart friend. Natural language gets pulled by AI better than a keyword list.
  5. 5WordPress: Open Yoast SEO or RankMath on the page, find the “Meta Description” field, and paste your description.
  6. 6Squarespace: Pages → gear icon → SEO → “SEO Description”.
  7. 7Custom HTML: add inside <head>: <meta name=“description” content=“Your description here.”>
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
View page source and search for “meta name=“description”“. Confirm your text appears in the content attribute. Re-run your free scan — meta description check should be green.
6

Add Schema.org Structured Data

Critical 🕑 30–45 minutes
Why This Matters
Schema.org structured data is JSON code you add to your page that explicitly tells AI crawlers: this is what my business is, this is where it is, this is what it does. Without it, AI has to infer everything from your text — which leads to vague or incorrect descriptions. With it, AI gets a machine-readable fact sheet about your business. This is one of the highest-impact fixes you can make.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Decide your schema type: Organization (any business), LocalBusiness (if you serve a geographic area), Product (if you sell a product), or Service. You can use more than one.
  2. 2Go to technicalseo.com/tools/schema-markup-generator/ and select your type. Fill in your business name, URL, description, address, phone, logo URL, and social media links.
  3. 3The tool generates a JSON-LD code block: <script type=“application/ld+json”>{ “@context”: “https://schema.org”, … }</script>. Copy the entire block.
  4. 4WordPress: Install “Schema & Structured Data for WP” plugin (free). Or paste directly into your theme’s header.php inside the <head> tag.
  5. 5Next.js: Add a <Script> component with type=“application/ld+json” and dangerouslySetInnerHTML.
  6. 6Squarespace or Wix: Settings → Advanced → Code Injection → Header, and paste the full <script> block.
  7. 7Validate at search.google.com/test/rich-results — paste your URL and confirm your schema type is detected with no errors.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
Run your URL through search.google.com/test/rich-results. Confirm your schema type appears with no critical errors. Re-run your free scan — the structured data check should turn green.
7

Make Sure Your Business Name Appears on the Page

Critical 🕑 15–20 minutes
Why This Matters
AI assistants learn your business name by reading it repeatedly in consistent contexts across the web. If your own homepage doesn’t mention your business name clearly, AI cannot build a confident association between your URL and your brand. The fix is simple: make sure your exact business name appears in your header, H1, and body text.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Check your page for your exact business name — the same name you use on Google Business Profile, Yelp, LinkedIn, and other directories. Inconsistent names confuse AI.
  2. 2Your business name should appear in at least 3 places: (1) your logo alt text, (2) your H1 or a prominent headline, and (3) your “About” or intro paragraph.
  3. 3Update your logo image alt text: in HTML find your <img> tag for the logo and add alt=“[Business Name] logo”. In WordPress, go to Media Library, click the logo, and update the Alt Text field.
  4. 4Update your header/navigation: make sure your business name appears as visible text, not just as an image with no alt tag.
  5. 5Add your business name to your homepage intro naturally — “At [Business Name], we…” This gives AI a clear entity-to-URL mapping.
  6. 6Check that the name is exactly consistent everywhere — same capitalisation, same spelling, no abbreviations. “River HVAC” and “RiverHVAC” are treated as two different entities.
  7. 7Add your business name to your <title> tag and meta description if it isn’t already there.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
Use Ctrl+F on your homepage to search for your business name. It should appear at least 3 times. Re-run your free scan with your business name in the optional field — the check should turn green.

8

Add Open Graph Tags

Recommended 🕑 20–30 minutes
Why This Matters
Open Graph tags (og:title, og:description, og:image) were created for social media sharing, but AI crawlers also use them as a secondary layer of context. When all three are present, AI has multiple consistent signals confirming what your business is. Missing OG tags is a fast fix that improves your trustworthiness signal with AI systems.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1You need 3 OG tags: og:title (same as your meta title), og:description (same as your meta description), og:image (a 1200×630px brand image).
  2. 2Create your og:image: use Canva (free) — search “Facebook Link Preview” which is 1200×630px. Add your logo, business name, and a clean background. Export as PNG.
  3. 3Upload your og:image to your website and copy the full URL (e.g. https://yourdomain.com/og-image.png).
  4. 4WordPress with Yoast: Go to Yoast → Social → Facebook and upload your og:image there. Yoast sets og:title and og:description automatically from your SEO fields.
  5. 5Custom HTML: add these 3 lines inside your <head>:
    <meta property=“og:title” content=“Your Business Name — Service”>
    <meta property=“og:description” content=“What you do, who you serve.”>
    <meta property=“og:image” content=“https://yourdomain.com/og-image.png”>
  6. 6Next.js: Add to your page’s metadata export with the openGraph key containing title, description, and images array.
  7. 7Test at opengraph.xyz — paste your URL and confirm all three preview correctly.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
View page source and search for “og:title”, “og:description”, “og:image” — all three should appear. Re-run your free scan to confirm the check turns green.
9

Add a Clear H1 Heading to Your Page

Recommended 🕑 10–15 minutes
Why This Matters
The H1 is the main headline of your page — it’s the first human-readable signal AI uses to understand what the page is about. A missing H1 means AI has to guess your page’s purpose. A strong H1 that includes your business name and what you do gives AI a clear, immediate answer. This is one of the fastest fixes with one of the most direct impacts on how AI describes you.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Use this formula: [What you do] for [who you serve] in [where, if local]. Example: “Trusted HVAC Repair for Austin Homeowners”. Be clear, not clever.
  2. 2You should have exactly one H1 per page. Multiple H1s confuse crawlers — they don’t know which one is the primary topic.
  3. 3WordPress: Edit the page in Gutenberg. Click the title block and confirm it is set to H1 in the block settings. Or in the text editor, ensure one line is wrapped in <h1> tags.
  4. 4Squarespace: Click the main text block on your homepage. Highlight the top line and set it to “Heading 1” in the text formatting toolbar.
  5. 5Custom HTML: Find your main headline and change the tag to <h1>. Make sure only this one heading uses <h1>.
  6. 6Make the H1 visible. Do not hide it with CSS (display:none or visibility:hidden). Hidden H1s are flagged as deceptive by AI crawlers.
  7. 7Check your heading hierarchy: one H1, then H2 for major sections, H3 for subsections. AI reads this hierarchy to understand your content structure.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
View page source and search for “<h1”. Confirm exactly one H1 tag exists with descriptive text. Re-run your free scan — the H1 check should turn green.
10

Add a Mobile Viewport Tag

Recommended 🕑 5 minutes
Why This Matters
The viewport meta tag tells browsers and crawlers that your site is designed to work on mobile devices. AI crawlers use mobile-friendliness as a proxy for site quality and maintenance. A missing viewport tag doesn’t just hurt mobile users — it signals to AI systems that your site may be outdated or poorly maintained. This is a one-line fix.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Open your site’s HTML source or template file.
  2. 2Find the <head> section.
  3. 3Add this line inside the <head> block:
    <meta name=“viewport” content=“width=device-width, initial-scale=1”>
  4. 4WordPress: Go to Appearance → Theme File Editor → header.php. Look for an existing viewport tag — if missing, add the line above just after the opening <head> tag.
  5. 5Squarespace / Wix / Shopify: This is handled automatically by the platform. If this check failed, a theme customisation likely removed it — contact their support.
  6. 6Next.js: Add viewport to your metadata export:
    export const metadata = { viewport: ‘width=device-width, initial-scale=1’ }
  7. 7Save and publish your changes.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
View page source and search for “viewport”. Confirm the tag appears in the <head>. Re-run your free scan — the viewport check should turn green.
11

Add a Canonical URL Tag

Recommended 🕑 5–10 minutes
Why This Matters
A canonical tag tells search engines and AI crawlers: “This is the definitive version of this page.” Without it, if your site is accessible at multiple URLs (with/without www, HTTP and HTTPS), AI crawlers may split their attention between them — weakening your authority signal. A canonical tag consolidates everything to one URL. This is a one-line fix.
How to Fix It — Step by Step
  1. 1Decide your canonical URL: the single “true” version of your homepage. Best practice: https://yourdomain.com/ (HTTPS, without www, with trailing slash).
  2. 2Add this tag inside your page’s <head>:
    <link rel=“canonical” href=“https://yourdomain.com/”>
  3. 3WordPress with Yoast SEO: This is set automatically from your site URL. Go to Settings → General → WordPress Address (URL) and confirm it matches your canonical URL.
  4. 4Next.js: Add to your metadata export:
    export const metadata = { alternates: { canonical: ‘https://yourdomain.com/’ } }
  5. 5Custom HTML: Paste the <link rel=“canonical”> tag directly inside your <head> block.
  6. 6Make sure every page on your site has its own canonical tag pointing to itself — not just the homepage.
  7. 7If you have duplicate pages (e.g. products in multiple category paths in Shopify), use canonical tags on duplicates pointing to the primary version.
✅
How to Confirm It’s Fixed
View page source and search for “canonical”. Confirm the tag appears pointing to your primary URL. Re-run your free scan — the canonical check should turn green.
