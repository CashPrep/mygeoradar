What This Guide Does

This guide walks WordPress users through every GEO fix available on the platform. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) means making your site easy for AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Bing Copilot to read, trust, and cite.

WordPress gives you more control than any other website builder covered in this series. You can edit every technical SEO setting, add full schema markup, control your robots.txt, manage crawl settings, and publish rich blog content. This makes WordPress the most powerful platform for GEO.

Step 1 — Fix Your Homepage Title and Meta Description

Your homepage title is the first thing AI systems read to understand what your site is about. It must be specific, not generic.

With Rank Math

  1. Go to your WordPress homepage (front page or blog page)
  2. Click Edit
  3. In the page editor, scroll down to the Rank Math SEO panel
  4. Click the Edit Snippet button
  5. Update the SEO Title field
  6. Update the Meta Description field
  7. Update the page

With Yoast SEO

  1. Open the homepage in the editor
  2. Scroll to the Yoast SEO box
  3. Click the SEO tab
  4. Update SEO title and Meta description
  5. Update the page

Step 3 — Set Up Schema Markup

WordPress is the best platform for schema markup because plugins handle it automatically for common types, and you can add custom JSON-LD for everything else.

Step 3A — Enable automatic schema in Rank Math

  1. Go to Rank Math → Titles & Meta
  2. Click the Posts tab
  3. Set Schema Type to Article for blog posts
  4. Click the Pages tab
  5. Set Schema Type based on what your pages are (WebPage, Service, etc.)
  6. Save

Step 3B — Add Organization schema to your homepage

  1. Open your homepage in the editor
  2. Scroll to the Rank Math SEO panel
  3. Click Schema (the star icon tab)
  4. Click Add New Schema → Organization
  5. Fill in your business name, URL, logo, description, and social URLs
  6. Save and update the page

Step 3C — Add FAQPage schema

  1. Open any page with a FAQ section
  2. In the Rank Math panel, click Schema → Add New Schema → FAQ
  3. Add each question and answer pair
  4. Update the page

Step 3D — Add custom JSON-LD manually (advanced)

  1. In the WordPress editor, add a Custom HTML block
  2. Paste your JSON-LD schema directly
  3. Update the page

Verify all schema

  1. Go to Google Rich Results Test
  2. Enter each key page URL and confirm schema appears with no errors

Step 4 — Optimize Your Blog for GEO

Blog content is your single highest-value GEO asset. AI tools actively pull from blog posts when forming answers — especially posts structured to answer specific questions.

Blog post structure for GEO

  1. H1 — The question or topic (your title)
  2. Opening paragraph — Direct 2–3 sentence answer (write this first)
  3. H2 — Background or context
  4. H2 — Step-by-step explanation or detailed breakdown
  5. H2 — Common mistakes or FAQ section
  6. Short closing paragraph or CTA
The opening paragraph matters most. AI systems often pull from the first 100 words of a post. Write a direct, quotable answer immediately after the title.

Step 5 — Add Author Bios to All Posts

AI systems favor content written by identified, credible authors. A missing or blank author bio is a trust signal gap that is easy to fix.

How to add an author bio in WordPress

  1. Go to Users → Your Profile in the WordPress dashboard
  2. Fill in the Biographical Info field with 2–4 sentences about your name, title, expertise area, and experience
  3. Upload a Profile Picture via Gravatar (gravatar.com with your email)
  4. Save

Display the bio on posts

  1. Go to Appearance → Theme Customizer
  2. Look for a “Post Meta” or “Author Bio” toggle and enable it

Or install the Simple Author Box plugin (free) which adds a clean author bio box to every post automatically.

Step 6 — Fix Image Alt Text

WordPress lets you add alt text to every image via the Media Library. Alt text helps AI crawlers and screen readers understand what images contain.

How to add alt text when uploading

  1. Go to Media → Add New
  2. Upload your image
  3. Click on the image to open its details
  4. Fill in the Alt Text field
  5. Save

How to fix existing images

  1. Go to Media → Library
  2. Click any image without alt text
  3. Add alt text in the right panel
  4. Update

Step 7 — Configure Your Sitemap and robots.txt

WordPress with an SEO plugin gives you full control over both your sitemap and robots.txt.

Sitemap with Rank Math

  1. Go to Rank Math → Sitemap Settings
  2. Enable sitemaps for Posts, Pages, and any custom post types
  3. Your sitemap is live at yoursite.com/sitemap_index.xml

Submit to Google Search Console

  1. Go to Google Search Console
  2. Click Sitemaps in the left menu
  3. Enter sitemap_index.xml and click Submit

robots.txt

  1. Go to Rank Math → General Settings → Edit robots.txt
  2. Make sure your important pages are not blocked
Critical: Make sure your robots.txt does NOT contain Disallow: / for user agents like GPTBot or PerplexityBot.

Step 8 — Improve Page Speed

Faster pages get crawled more completely, which directly impacts GEO visibility.

Install a caching plugin

  1. Go to Plugins → Add New
  2. Search for WP Super Cache or W3 Total Cache (both free)
  3. Install, activate, and follow the setup wizard

Compress and optimize images

  1. Install the Smush plugin (free tier available)
  2. Go to Smush → Bulk Smush
  3. Click Bulk Smush Now

Check your speed

  1. Go to PageSpeed Insights
  2. Enter your URL and fix any red or orange issues

Step 9 — Build Strong Trust Pages

Your About and Contact pages are trust signals that AI systems use to verify your business is real.

About page — must include

  • Your name or founder name
  • What the business does and who it helps
  • Where you are based
  • A real origin story (2–3 sentences minimum)
  • Your credentials or experience in this area
  • Contact info or link to contact page

Contact page — must include

  • Email address
  • Phone number (if applicable)
  • Physical address or service area
  • Contact form
  • Business hours and social links

Your business Name, Address, and Phone must match exactly across your site, Google Business Profile, Bing Places, and any directory listing.

Step 10 — Build Off-Site Citations and Reviews

AI tools cross-reference your website against other trusted sources. Consistent, credible mentions increase your citation likelihood.

Priority listings to create

  1. Google Business Profile — business.google.com — free and essential
  2. Bing Places for Business — bingplaces.com — feeds Bing/Copilot directly
  3. Trustpilot — create a profile and collect text reviews
  4. LinkedIn Company Page — link from your site footer
  5. Better Business Bureau — high trust signal for AI
  6. Niche directories — find 2–3 industry-specific directories
