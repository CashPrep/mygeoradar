Step 1 — Fix Your Homepage Title and Meta Description

Your homepage has to tell AI systems exactly what your business is, who it serves, and where it operates.

  1. Open your Wix dashboard
  2. Go to the SEO settings for the homepage
  3. Update the SEO title using the formula: [What you do] | [Brand Name] | [Location or Niche]
  4. Update the meta description: say what you offer, who it helps, and what result they get
  5. Save and publish your site

Example title: AI SEO Visibility Audits | MyGeoRadar | GEO Optimization for Small Businesses

Example description: MyGeoRadar helps businesses improve AI search visibility with GEO audits, actionable fixes, and platform-specific guides.

Step 2 — Rewrite Key Pages for AI Readability

AI tools prefer pages that answer questions clearly. Your homepage, services, product pages, and about page should explain what you do in plain language.

Use this page structure:

  • First paragraph: Say exactly what the page is about in 2–3 sentences
  • Second paragraph: Explain who it is for
  • Third section: Bullets with features, use cases, or deliverables
  • Final section: Trust details like location, experience, or pricing model

Weak: "We help brands grow online with innovative digital experiences."

Better: "MyGeoRadar helps businesses improve visibility in AI search tools like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, and Perplexity. The platform scans your site, identifies missing GEO signals, and shows what to fix."

Step 3 — Add Structured Data in Wix

Structured data tells search engines and AI systems what a page represents.

  1. Open the page you want to optimize
  2. Go to that page's SEO settings
  3. Open the advanced section
  4. Find the structured data markup area
  5. Add new markup or convert preset markup to custom if needed
  6. Save and publish

Start with these schema types:

  • Organization for your homepage
  • LocalBusiness if you serve a local market
  • Article for blog posts
  • FAQPage for FAQ sections
  • Product for product pages

Simple homepage schema example:

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Organization",
  "name": "MyGeoRadar",
  "url": "https://mygeoradar.com",
  "description": "AI visibility audit and GEO optimization platform."
}

After publishing, test the page using Google's Rich Results Test.

Step 4 — Set Up Blog Content That Answers Real Questions

Wix supports blog content and post-level SEO settings. Helpful blog posts give AI systems specific language to quote and evidence that your site is authoritative.

Structure every post like this:

  1. Title = the question or comparison
  2. First paragraph = direct answer
  3. H2 sections = explanation, steps, examples, mistakes
  4. Final section = short recap and CTA

For every Wix blog post:

  • Add a clear SEO title
  • Write a custom meta description
  • Add a featured image
  • Add descriptive alt text to every image
  • Assign categories and tags

Step 5 — Improve Image SEO and Alt Text

Wix supports adding alt text to images in the editor and blog workflows. Alt text improves accessibility and gives images more context for AI crawlers.

Alt text rules:

  • Describe what is actually shown
  • Mention the product, service, or context when relevant
  • Do not stuff keywords unnaturally
  • Keep it short and factual

Bad: image1

Better: Dashboard showing an AI visibility audit report for a small business website

Step 6 — Connect Google Search Console

  1. Go to Google Search Console
  2. Add your website as a property
  3. Verify ownership using the method Wix supports in your dashboard
  4. Submit your sitemap
  5. Check indexing and page coverage regularly

Step 7 — Strengthen Your About and Contact Pages

AI systems are more likely to trust sites that clearly identify who is behind them.

Add these details:

  • Founder or company name
  • What the business does and who it helps
  • Location or service area
  • Contact email and form
  • Social links if active
  • Real business background or story

Step 8 — Create Better Service or Product Pages

Each important page should target one clear topic. For every service page include: a clear page title, a direct opening paragraph, a "Who this is for" section, a "What's included" section, FAQs near the bottom, and proof or examples.

Step 9 — Publish FAQ Content on Important Pages

FAQ sections mirror how users ask questions in AI tools and create natural opportunities for FAQPage schema.

Add 4–8 FAQs to:

  • Homepage
  • Pricing page
  • Product or service pages
  • Guide sales page

Write each answer in plain English, 2–5 sentences each.

Step 10 — Build Off-Site Trust Signals

Your Wix website becomes more citable when other trusted sources confirm your business exists.

  • Google Business Profile
  • Bing Places
  • Trustpilot or other review platform
  • LinkedIn company page
  • Relevant niche directories

Keep your name, URL, and contact details consistent everywhere.
