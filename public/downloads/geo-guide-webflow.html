MyGeoRadar Platform-Specific Playbook — mygeoradar.com

What This Guide Does

This guide walks Webflow users through every GEO fix available on the platform. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) means making your site readable, trustworthy, and citable by AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Bing Copilot.

Webflow gives you more control than Squarespace or Wix — full SEO settings per page, custom code injection, robots.txt editing, sitemap control, and CMS field binding for dynamic schema. This makes it one of the strongest platforms for GEO without writing custom code.

Step 1 — Fix Your Homepage Title and Meta Description

Your homepage title is the first signal AI systems use to understand your site. It must be specific and descriptive, not generic.

How to update in Webflow

  1. Open your project in the Webflow Designer
  2. Click on the Pages panel (top left)
  3. Hover over your homepage and click the gear icon (Page Settings)
  4. Update the Title Tag field
  5. Update the Meta Description field
  6. Click Save and republish

Title formula

[What the page covers] | [Brand Name]

Example: AI GEO Visibility Audits | MyGeoRadar

Meta description formula

What do you offer, who is it for, what result do they get? Keep it 150–160 characters.

Example: MyGeoRadar scans your site and shows exactly what to fix to appear in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. Free GEO score in 60 seconds.

Step 2 — Add SEO Settings to Every Key Page

Webflow lets you set a unique title and description for every page. Repeat the process from Step 1 for each page in your Pages panel.

Pages to update in order

  1. Homepage
  2. Services or product pages
  3. About page
  4. Contact page
  5. Pricing page
  6. Any landing pages

For CMS collection pages (blog posts)

  1. Go to CMS → Collections → Blog Posts
  2. Click into any blog post
  3. Fill in the SEO Title and Meta Description fields
  4. Save and publish
Pro tip: In Webflow CMS, you can bind the SEO Title field to your post Title field so it auto-fills for every new post. Set this up in the Collection Template page settings.

Step 3 — Add Schema Markup

Webflow gives you full control over schema markup. You can add static JSON-LD for the whole site, page-specific schema via custom code, or dynamic CMS-bound schema for blog posts.

Step 3A — Add Organization schema to your homepage

  1. In the Designer, open your homepage
  2. Go to Page Settings → Custom Code → Head Code
  3. Paste your Organization schema:
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Organization",
  "name": "Your Business Name",
  "url": "https://yoursite.com",
  "logo": "https://yoursite.com/logo.png",
  "description": "One sentence describing what your business does.",
  "email": "hello@yoursite.com",
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.linkedin.com/company/your-company",
    "https://twitter.com/yourhandle"
  ]
}
</script>
  1. Save and publish

Step 3B — Add Article schema to blog posts (CMS bound)

  1. Open your Blog Post Collection Template in the Designer
  2. Go to Page Settings → Custom Code → Head Code
  3. Paste the Article schema using CMS field bindings:
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Article",
  "headline": "{{wf {"path":"name"} }}",
  "datePublished": "{{wf {"path":"published-on","type":"Date"} }}",
  "author": {
    "@type": "Person",
    "name": "Your Name"
  },
  "publisher": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "Your Business Name",
    "url": "https://yoursite.com"
  }
}
</script>
  1. Save and publish

Step 3C — Add FAQPage schema to pages with FAQ sections

  1. Open the specific page in the Designer
  2. Go to Page Settings → Custom Code → Head Code
  3. Paste your FAQPage JSON-LD with your real questions and answers
  4. Save and publish

Verify all schema

  1. Go to Google Rich Results Test
  2. Enter each key page URL
  3. Confirm schema types appear with no errors

Step 4 — Optimize Your Blog for GEO

Blog content is your highest-value GEO asset. AI tools actively pull from well-structured blog posts when forming answers.

Blog post structure for GEO

  1. Title (H1) — the question or topic
  2. Opening paragraph — direct 2–3 sentence answer (write this first)
  3. H2 section — background or context
  4. H2 section — step-by-step or detailed breakdown
  5. H2 section — FAQ or common questions
  6. Short closing paragraph
The opening paragraph matters most. AI systems often pull from the first 100 words of a post. Write a direct, quotable answer immediately after the title.

Step 5 — Fix Image Alt Text

Webflow lets you add alt text to every image element in the Designer. This is a quick but high-impact fix.

How to add alt text in Webflow

  1. Click on any image element in the Designer
  2. In the right panel, find the Alt Text field
  3. Write a clear description of the image
  4. Republish

For CMS images

  1. Go to your CMS collection
  2. Click into each item
  3. Find the image field and add alt text in the field settings
  4. Save

Step 6 — Configure robots.txt

Webflow gives you full robots.txt editing — one of the best features for GEO compared to other visual builders.

How to edit robots.txt in Webflow

  1. Go to Project Settings → SEO
  2. Scroll to the Robots.txt section
  3. Edit the robots.txt content directly
  4. Save and republish
Critical: Make sure your robots.txt does NOT block AI crawlers like GPTBot, PerplexityBot, or Googlebot. If they cannot crawl your site, you cannot be cited.

A safe default robots.txt:

User-agent: *
Allow: /

Sitemap: https://yoursite.com/sitemap.xml

Step 7 — Submit Your Sitemap

Webflow automatically generates a sitemap at yoursite.com/sitemap.xml. Submit it to Google Search Console.

How to submit your sitemap

  1. Go to Google Search Console
  2. Select your property
  3. Click Sitemaps in the left sidebar
  4. Enter sitemap.xml and click Submit

Confirm sitemap is enabled in Webflow

  1. Go to Project Settings → SEO
  2. Make sure Auto-generate sitemap is enabled
  3. Save

Step 8 — Open Graph and Social Tags

Webflow automatically generates Open Graph tags from your page SEO title and description. Make sure these are filled in for every key page (see Step 1 and 2).

Set a default OG image

  1. Go to Project Settings → SEO
  2. Upload a Default Open Graph Image (1200x630px)
  3. Save and republish

Set page-specific OG images

  1. Open the page in the Designer
  2. Go to Page Settings → SEO
  3. Upload a specific Open Graph Image for that page
  4. Save and republish

Step 9 — Build Strong Trust Pages

Your About and Contact pages are trust signals that AI systems use to verify your business is real and legitimate.

About page — must include

  • Your name or founder name
  • What the business does and who it helps
  • Where you are based
  • A real origin story (2–3 sentences minimum)
  • Your credentials or experience
  • Contact info or link to contact page

Contact page — must include

  • Email address
  • Phone number (if applicable)
  • Physical address or service area
  • Contact form
  • Social links

Your business Name, Address, and Phone must match exactly across your site, Google Business Profile, Bing Places, and any directory listings.

Step 10 — Build Off-Site Citations and Reviews

AI tools cross-reference your website against other trusted sources. Consistent, credible mentions of your business across the web increase citation likelihood.

Priority listings to create

  1. Google Business Profile — business.google.com — free and essential
  2. Bing Places for Business — bingplaces.com — feeds Bing/Copilot directly
  3. Trustpilot — create a profile and collect text reviews
  4. LinkedIn Company Page — link from your site footer
  5. Better Business Bureau — high trust signal for AI
  6. Niche directories — find 2–3 industry-specific directories
