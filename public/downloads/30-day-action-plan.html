How to use this calendar

  • Do one task per day. Don't batch or skip ahead — each builds on the last.
  • Every task has a "done" condition. If you can't answer "yes" to it, the task isn't done.
  • If a task takes you longer than 60 minutes, stop and pick it up tomorrow. Momentum matters more than perfection.
  • "+1" tasks are optional. Do them if you have an extra 20 minutes that day.
Week 1 — Foundations Days 1–5
1
Define your NAP source of truth
Write your exact business name, address (with suite), phone number, and website URL in a document. This is now your canonical reference. Every platform must match it exactly. Print it out and tape it somewhere visible.
+1: Search Google for your business name and audit every result for NAP inconsistencies
Done when: Your NAP doc is written and saved somewhere permanent
2
Claim and verify Google Business Profile
Go to business.google.com. Claim your listing. Start verification. If already claimed, check that it's verified (not just claimed). Note: postcard verification takes 5–14 days — start it today even if you move on to other tasks.
+1: Check for and merge any duplicate GBP listings you find
Done when: Verification is initiated or already complete
3
Complete every GBP field
Primary and secondary categories, description (use the formula from the playbook), services list, hours including holidays, attributes, and a minimum of 10 photos. This single task has the highest ROI of anything in this calendar.
+1: Enable GBP messaging if you can commit to replying within 24 hours
Done when: Every field has content — zero blank fields
4
Claim Bing Places and Apple Maps
Go to bingplaces.com and mapsconnect.apple.com. Claim both listings. Fill them out with your canonical NAP. Bing feeds Microsoft Copilot. Apple Maps feeds Siri. Both are almost universally skipped by small businesses.
Done when: Both listings are claimed and filled with your correct NAP
5
Claim and complete Yelp, Facebook, and BBB
Three platforms in one day. Go to biz.yelp.com, business.facebook.com, and bbb.org. Claim each listing. Fill in name, address, phone, website, and description using your canonical NAP. Even partial completion is a win today.
+1: Also claim your Foursquare listing at business.foursquare.com
Done when: All three are claimed with correct NAP
🏁

Week 1 milestone: Entity foundation set

Your business now has a consistent, verified presence on the platforms that matter most. AI assistants are already picking up better signals.

Week 2 — Structured Data Days 6–10
6
Add LocalBusiness schema to your homepage
Copy the LocalBusiness JSON-LD template from the playbook. Fill in all fields with your canonical info. Add it to your homepage <head>. Use a plugin if you're on WordPress. Validate at search.google.com/test/rich-results before moving on.
Done when: Schema is live and passes validation with zero critical errors
7
Add Organization schema to your About page
Copy the Organization JSON-LD template from the playbook. Fill in name, URL, logo, foundingDate, description, founder, and sameAs links. Add it to your About page <head>. Validate. Fix any errors before continuing.
Done when: Schema is live on About page and passes validation
8
Write and add FAQPage schema
Write 4–6 Q&A pairs: what you do, where you're located, how long you've been in business, what makes you different, what your hours are, how to contact you. Add FAQPage JSON-LD to your About or FAQ page. Validate.
+1: Add the FAQ questions as visible text on the page too — this improves both human readability and AI crawlability
Done when: FAQPage schema is live and validated with 4+ questions
9
Set up Google Search Console and submit your sitemap
Go to search.google.com/search-console. Add your domain property. Verify ownership. Go to Sitemaps and submit your sitemap URL (usually yoursite.com/sitemap.xml). Check for any existing crawl errors and note them.
Done when: Property is verified and sitemap is submitted with no errors
10
Set up Bing Webmaster Tools
Go to bing.com/webmasters. Sign in with a Microsoft account. Import from Google Search Console (easiest) or add manually. Submit your sitemap. This is the single most-skipped technical step — and it powers Microsoft Copilot.
Done when: Bing Webmaster is live and sitemap submitted
⚡

Week 2 milestone: Structured data complete

AI crawlers can now read exactly who you are from structured sources you control. Perplexity and Gemini are already seeing the difference.

Week 3 — Content & Entity Clarity Days 11–20
11
Rewrite your About page using the AI formula
Follow the 4-part formula from the playbook: The What (2–3 sentences), The Credentials (bullet list), The Facts (dates, numbers, addresses), The Human (founder story). Remove all vague mission statement language. Every sentence should be a quotable fact.
Done when: About page has been rewritten with zero vague language
12
Create your /facts page
Create a new page at yourwebsite.com/facts (or /about/facts). Fill in every field from the playbook template: legal name, founded, founder, headquarters, service area, services, certifications, awards, media mentions, contact. Publish it. Make sure it's in your sitemap.
Done when: /facts page is live, indexed, and contains complete business information
13
Update your homepage to clearly state what you do, for whom, and where
Your homepage headline and first paragraph should answer: what type of business is this, what city/area is it in, and who is it for. No metaphors. If a stranger read your homepage in 5 seconds, they should know exactly what you do.
Done when: Homepage passes the "5-second stranger test" — anyone can identify what you do and where
14
Write one service page targeting "[your service] in [your city]"
Create or update a page for your most important service. Title: "[Service] in [City] — [Business Name]." Include: what the service is, who it's for, why choose you, your address and service area, and a clear call to action. 400–600 words minimum.
+1: Add a FAQ section at the bottom answering the top 3 questions customers ask about this service
Done when: Page is live with correct location signals and service description
15
Submit to 5 more directories
By now you should have Google, Bing, Apple, Yelp, Facebook, and BBB. Today: Yellow Pages (yp.com), Foursquare (business.foursquare.com), Nextdoor (business.nextdoor.com), Hotfrog (hotfrog.com), and one industry-specific directory for your category.
Done when: 5 new directories claimed with consistent NAP
16
Create your LinkedIn Company Page
Go to linkedin.com/company/setup/new. Create a company page with your exact business name, description, website, and location. Upload your logo. Add it to the sameAs array in your Organization schema (update and re-validate). LinkedIn is a high-authority entity signal for AI.
Done when: LinkedIn page is live and URL is added to your schema
17
Add your Google review link to your email signature and invoices
Go to your GBP dashboard → Get more reviews → copy the link. Add it to: your email signature, your invoice footer, your receipt template, and your follow-up email. This is the easiest compounding review system you can set up.
Done when: Review link is in at least 2 places customers regularly see
18
Send review requests to your last 20 customers
Look through your last 3–6 months of invoices, emails, or CRM entries. Identify 20 satisfied customers. Send each a personal 2-sentence text or email: "Hey [Name] — thanks for your business. If you have 2 minutes, a Google review would mean a lot: [link]."
Done when: 20 review request messages sent
19
Respond to every existing Google review you haven't responded to
Go through your GBP and respond to every unanswered review — positive and negative. Responses contain keywords and context AI can use. A 3-sentence response for a positive review is fine. For negative reviews: acknowledge, don't argue, offer to resolve offline.
Done when: Zero unanswered Google reviews remain
20
Run your first AI audit
Open ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude. For each one, ask: "Tell me everything you know about [Business] in [City]." Screenshot every response. Note: were you mentioned, was the info accurate, and are competitors shown instead? Save this as your Day 1 baseline.
Done when: 4 screenshots saved and your biggest gap identified
💪

Week 3 milestone: Content & authority base set

You now have complete, AI-quotable content and an active review strategy running. Most businesses never get this far.

Week 4 — Authority & Finalization Days 21–30
21
Identify and fix any NAP inconsistencies you found
Search "[Business Name] + [City]" and "[Business Name] + [old address]" on Google. Find every listing with wrong info. Log it. Correct the ones you can access today (start with the highest-authority ones: Yelp, Facebook, BBB).
Done when: All accessible inconsistencies are corrected and logged
22
Sign up for HARO / Connectively and respond to your first query
Go to connectively.us and create a free source account. You'll receive daily emails with journalist queries. Find one relevant to your industry and respond with a thoughtful, quotable answer. Include your name, business, city, and website. One successful HARO citation can meaningfully lift your AI authority.
Done when: Account created and at least one query response sent
23
Join your local chamber of commerce
Search "[Your City] chamber of commerce." Most charge $150–$400/year. The member directory listing alone is worth it for AI visibility — chambers have high-authority, well-indexed domains. Ask to be featured in the member spotlight newsletter.
Done when: Membership application submitted and directory listing requested
24
Email your local business journalist with a story pitch
Find your city's local newspaper or business journal. Find the email of the business reporter (usually on their byline). Pitch a genuine story: new milestone, unusual business model, community initiative, notable achievement. One short email. Keep it 5 sentences or less.
+1: Also submit a press release to PRNewswire or a free PR distribution service
Done when: Story pitch email is sent to at least one journalist
25
Follow up on Day 18 review requests
Send a single follow-up to everyone from Day 18 who hasn't left a review. Message: "Hey [Name] — just following up on my earlier note. If you have a moment, here's the link: [link]. No worries if not — just appreciate your time!" Never send more than one follow-up.
Done when: Follow-up messages sent to non-responders from Day 18
26
Submit to 3 industry-specific directories
Research the top directories for your specific industry. Examples: legal (Avvo, Martindale, Justia), medical (Healthgrades, Zocdoc, WebMD), home services (Angi, HomeAdvisor, Thumbtack), restaurants (TripAdvisor, OpenTable), software (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot). Submit to 3.
Done when: 3 industry directories submitted with correct NAP
27
Use the Prompt Pack to run a full 10-prompt AI audit
Open the Prompt Pack PDF. Run all 10 prompts across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude. This takes about 25 minutes. Screenshot everything. Compare to your Day 20 baseline. You should already see improvement in at least 1–2 of the four assistants.
Done when: All 10 prompts run across all 4 assistants and screenshots saved
28
Fix any hallucinations or inaccuracies found in the Day 27 audit
For every wrong fact an AI stated: correct it on GBP, correct it on every directory that has the wrong version, update your schema if needed, add the correct fact to your /facts page. Prioritize the most harmful inaccuracies first (wrong address, wrong category, wrong phone).
Done when: Every identified inaccuracy has been corrected at its source
29
Review your 27-point checklist and close any remaining gaps
Go through the AI Visibility Checklist PDF item by item. Any unchecked box is a gap. Prioritize the ones in Sections A and B (entity + structured data) — they have the highest impact. Try to close at least 3 unchecked items today.
Done when: Checklist reviewed and at least 3 gaps closed
30
Schedule your quarterly maintenance habits
Open your calendar. Create 4 recurring events: (1) Weekly — respond to new reviews. (2) Monthly — check GSC for errors, add one piece of new content. (3) Quarterly — run full 10-prompt AI audit, update /facts page. (4) Annually — audit all directory listings, pursue 1–2 new editorial mentions.
+1: Send yourself a 90-day reminder email with a link to your Day 20 screenshots as your baseline comparison
Done when: All 4 recurring calendar events are set
🏆

Day 30: You're done. Now maintain.

You've built an AI visibility foundation that most businesses will never have. Run the quarterly audit every 90 days and you'll compound your advantage over time.
