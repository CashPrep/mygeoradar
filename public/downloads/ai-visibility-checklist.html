Section 1

Entity & Basics — 8 Items

1. Google Business Profile is claimed and verified Critical
Verify: Sign in to business.google.com — your listing shows “Verified” status (green checkmark).
2. GBP description is complete (750 characters, uses the NAP formula) Critical
Verify: Description field in GBP is filled, includes business type, city/state, 2–3 services, and one credibility statement.
3. GBP primary + secondary categories set correctly Critical
Verify: Primary category is the most specific possible match. At least 2 secondary categories are added.
4. GBP has at least 10 photos High
Verify: Photos section in GBP shows 10+ images including exterior, interior, team, and work examples.
5. GBP Services / Products section is filled out High
Verify: At least 5 services or products are listed with names and descriptions.
6. Website has HTTPS and loads without errors Critical
Verify: URL starts with https://. No browser security warnings. Homepage loads fully.
7. NAP source of truth document is defined Critical
Verify: You have a written record of the exact business name, address, phone, and website URL format you will use everywhere.
8. NAP on website matches GBP exactly Critical
Verify: Copy the address from your website footer/contact page and compare character-by-character with your GBP listing.
Section 2

Structured Data — 5 Items

9. LocalBusiness schema is live on the homepage Critical
Verify: Go to search.google.com/test/rich-results, enter your homepage URL — it detects a LocalBusiness schema with no critical errors.
10. Schema @type is the most specific match (not just “LocalBusiness”) High
Verify: Check schema.org/LocalBusiness subtypes. If you’re a restaurant, dentist, or contractor, use the specific subtype — not the generic parent type.
11. Organization schema is live on the About page High
Verify: Rich Results Test on your About page URL detects an Organization schema.
12. FAQPage schema is live on at least one page High
Verify: Rich Results Test detects a FAQPage schema. FAQs answer the questions customers most commonly ask you.
13. Schema sameAs array links to all major profiles Medium
Verify: Your LocalBusiness schema’s sameAs array includes links to GBP, Yelp, Facebook, and any other major active profiles.
Section 3

Citations & Reviews — 7 Items

14. Bing Places listing claimed and complete Critical
Verify: Search your business on Bing Maps — your listing appears with correct name, address, and phone.
15. Apple Maps listing claimed and complete High
Verify: Search your business on Apple Maps (from an iPhone or maps.apple.com) — correct listing appears.
16. Yelp, Facebook, and BBB listings are claimed and accurate High
Verify: Search your business on each platform and confirm NAP matches your source of truth exactly.
17. At least 10 total directory citations exist with consistent NAP High
Verify: Run a free scan on BrightLocal or Moz Local — at least 10 directories list your business with matching NAP.
18. No conflicting old addresses or phone numbers exist Critical
Verify: Search your old address or phone number in Google — no active directory listings should surface the outdated information.
19. At least 15 Google reviews with an average above 4.0 High
Verify: Your GBP listing shows 15+ reviews and a star rating of 4.0 or higher.
20. Reviews exist on at least 2 platforms (not just Google) Medium
Verify: Check Yelp and/or Facebook for at least 3–5 reviews each.
Section 4

Content & Authority — 5 Items

21. About page uses the AI-optimized formula (What / Credentials / Facts / Human) Critical
Verify: Your About page states your business type, location, services, founding year, and at least one credibility fact in the first two paragraphs.
22. A /facts page (or equivalent) is live on the website High
Verify: yourwebsite.com/facts (or /about/facts or similar) loads and contains a structured list of key business facts — founding date, services, licenses, awards, location.
23. At least one editorial mention from a third-party source exists High
Verify: Google your business name — at least one result is a third-party article, press mention, or directory that you did not create (local news, industry blog, chamber feature).
24. Website has at least one service or location page targeting a “best X in Y” query Medium
Verify: You have a page (could be homepage, service page, or blog post) that directly answers “[service] in [city]” in its heading and body text.
25. Industry-specific niche directory listing exists (if applicable) Medium
Verify: If you’re a lawyer (Avvo), doctor (Healthgrades), contractor (Houzz/Angi), or similar — your niche platform listing is claimed and accurate.
Section 5

Technical & Monitoring — 4 Items

26. Google Search Console is set up and sitemap submitted Critical
Verify: Sign in to search.google.com/search-console — your property is verified and sitemap shows “Success” status.
27. Bing Webmaster Tools is set up and sitemap submitted High
Verify: Sign in to bing.com/webmasters — your site is verified and sitemap is submitted.

How to interpret your score

Count how many items you’ve checked off.

0–9
Invisible to AI. Start with Section 1 & 2 immediately.
10–18
Partially visible. AI may mention you rarely. Complete Section 3 & 4 next.
19–27
Strong foundation. Consistent AI mentions become much more likely for relevant queries.

Re-run this checklist every 90 days. Things change — your GBP can become unclaimed, citations can become inaccurate, reviews go stale. Quarterly checks keep your AI visibility compounding instead of eroding.
